Departments are the top-level organizational units in my-company-llm. They group related agents together and enable intelligent message routing.

## Creating Departments

Departments can be created through the API:

```bash
curl -X POST http://localhost:8000/api/departments \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "name": "Marketing",
    "description": "Handles brand, content, and social media"
  }'
```

They are also auto-created when you assign an agent to a new department name.

## Department Structure

Each department can contain:

- **One lead agent** — the primary point of contact for the department
- **Multiple worker agents** — specialists that report to the lead

When the CEO chat router identifies a message for a department, it routes to the lead agent. The lead can then delegate subtasks to workers.

## Department Properties

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| `name` | Department name (e.g., "Engineering", "Marketing") |
| `description` | What the department handles |
| `created_at` | When the department was created |

## Best Practices

- **Mirror your real org** — Create departments that match your actual organizational structure for intuitive management.
- **Keep departments focused** — Each department should have a clear responsibility area so the router can accurately direct messages.
- **Assign a lead** — Every department should have a lead agent to serve as the primary entry point.
- **Specialize workers** — Give worker agents narrow, specific roles within the department for better task execution.

## Office Map

The **Office Map** page provides a visual representation of your departments. Each department appears as a room containing its agents, making it easy to see the overall structure of your AI workforce at a glance.
